WWE Network

Rebuilt from the ground up

Overall

The WWE Network is a global streaming service used to showcase a 24 hour live streaming channel as well as On Demand content from the archive of World Wrestling Entertainment. Launched in 2014 the network was seen as in dire need of an update and this was publically announced in April 2019

Client

World Wrestling Entertainment

Project Date

2019

Role

Senior UX/UI Designer (contractor)

Introduction - A project in process

I was brought into this project at a precarious time. The project had been in design development for some time and was starting to hit a critical time. Devlopers had a strong framework of the updated app but the approach was one of release first improve later.

With my experience in TV interfaces I was brought in to help guide the product to completion and delivery and also to start work on the next phase of design.

Sign in

The sign-in process was created as a fast way to get existing customers into the content that they wanted to see. We created individual journeys for and split it into three different types of customers; People without any WWE account, People who have a WWE account including previous subscribers, and people who had an active subscription. This allowed targeted messaging in relation to a customer connect with the WWE Network.

As WWE is a global company, and the WWE Network was available in many regions, the payment process is one area that had a lot of intricacies. We had to account for different laws across the globe and also different privacies and regulations with user data following the arrival of GDPR regulations.
